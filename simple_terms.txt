Let's Spot the Kids Who Need Help EARLY

You know how when you're sick, it's way better if the doctor catches it early instead of waiting until you're really really sick?
Same idea here — but for learning.
Some kids start struggling with reading very early, but nobody notices until Grade 3 or 4, by which point it's much harder to help them. So this project asks: can we look at a kid on their very first day of kindergarten and make a pretty good guess about who might need extra help later?
We look at things like — how many words do they know? Can they hear the sounds in words? What's their home life like? — and we use those clues to spot kids who might need support before they fall behind.
AND — super importantly — we don't just say "this kid is at risk." We also explain exactly WHY. Like: "this kid was flagged because their phonics skills are low AND their class is very big AND they have less support at home." So teachers actually know what to do about it, not just who to worry about.
